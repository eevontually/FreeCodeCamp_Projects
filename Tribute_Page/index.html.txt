<h2 class="timeline-title">Timeline of Robin Williams' life:</h2> 

<p>
<ul>
<li><b>1951</b> - Born in Chicago, in the state of Illinois.</li>
<li><b>1967</b> - Moves to Marina Williams County, California.</li>
<li><b>1970</b> - Starts as a stand-up comedian in San Francisco and Los Angeles.</li>
<li><b>1977</b> - Appears in a low-budget comedy Can I Do It 'Till I Need Glasses?.</li>
<li><b>1978</b> - Marries his first wife, Valerie Velardi.</li>
<li><b>1979</b> - Wins first Golden Globe Award with best TV actor in "Mork and Mindy".</li>
<li><b>1980</b> - Stars in his first starring role in the children comedy film Popeye, directed by Robert Altman.</li>
<li><b>1983</b> - Birth of his first son, Zachary Pym "Zak" Williams.</li>
<li><b>1986</b> - Teams up with Whoopi Goldberg and Billy Crystal to fund Comic Relief USA, an annual HBO television benefit devoted to the homeless.</li>
<li><b>1988</b> - Divorces her first wife, Velardi.</li>
<li><b>1989</b> - Marries Marsha Garces, Zachary's nanny, who was pregnant with his child.</li>
<li><b>1990</b> - Nominated for best actor Oscar for "Dead Poets Society."</li>
<li><b>1998</b> - Wins an Oscar for best actor for his memorable performance in the film Good Will Hunting.</li>
<li><b>2005</b> - Receives the Cecil B. DeMille Award for career achievement from the Hollywood Foreign Press Association.</li>
<li><b>2006</b> - Seeks treatment for alcohol dependency.</li>
<li><b>2009</b> - Goes through heart surgery to replace his aortic valve.</li>
<li><b>2014</b> - Dies by suicide at the age of 63.</li>
</ul>
</p>
</div>
